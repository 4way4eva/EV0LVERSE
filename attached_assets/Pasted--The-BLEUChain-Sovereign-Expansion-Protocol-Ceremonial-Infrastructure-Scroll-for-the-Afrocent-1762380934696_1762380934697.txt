## III. ENFT Classes and Metadata Lifecycle

### 1. ENFT Standard Selection and Interoperability

BLEUChain operationalizes the concept of **Eternal Non-Fungible Tokens (ENFTs)**, extending NFT standards (ERC-721, ERC-1155, ERC-998) for cultural permanence, composability, and cross-sector translatability.

- **ERC-721 ENFTs:** Unique, indivisible ritual artifacts—one scroll, one hero, one codex.
- **ERC-1155 ENFTs:** Collections, multi-edition ceremonial items, or items with both fungible (energy, harvest, currency) and non-fungible (identity, scroll) aspects.
- **ERC-998/Composable ENFTs:** Bundled ritual assets—e.g., a kingdom ENFT containing heroes, relics, technologies, and land deeds; entire storyworlds can be transferred as sovereign inheritance packages.

The chosen standard depends on the intent:
- Unique script or ceremonial relic = ERC-721
- Harvested crops, educational tokens, or battle elixirs = ERC-1155 (semi-fungible)
- Layered city or dynasty with owned characters, scripts, and devices = ERC-998

### 2. ENFT Metadata, Lifecycle, and Dynamic Extension

Every ENFT on BLEUChain carries metadata conforming to best practices, with JSON schema extensions for ritual and restitution attributes. Key fields include:

- `origin_lineage`: ties ENFT to real historical/ancestral group.
- `ceremonial_phase`: state in the ritual lifecycle (initiated, in performance, vault, restored).
- `restitution_marker`: boolean plus array of restitution events (see below).
- `traits`: unique attributes (rarity, power, sacredness, participation requirements).
- `documentation_url`: off-chain archive or academic verification pointer.
- `animation_url/image/audio`: links to multimedia elements for AR/VR, metaverse, or transmedia use.

**Lifecycle Events:**
- Minting (by rightsholders/lineage/creator/AI ritual engine).
- Ritual deployment (used in a ceremony or media event).
- Attribution and tribute (ENFT linked to specific person or lineage for reward/restoration tracking).
- Mutation or Splitting (collaborative remixing for academy or gaming).
- Sealing and Vaulting (decommissioned, archived, or time-locked for future generations).

Dynamic ENFTs may allow metadata updates only through protocol-governed ceremonies (renaissance festivals, restitution cycles, or diaspora votes), and always with an auditable trail.
